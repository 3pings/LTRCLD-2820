Step {{ step }} - Explore the '{{data.apic_stdnt_uname}}' tenant details

Expand Application Profiles and Applicaton EPGs under kubernetes. Click on kubernetes.

Click on the Topology view that shows the EPGs and Contracts

Explore the relationship between the EGPs by clicking on 'kube-default' in the image

Cisco ACI uses EPGs (End Point Groups - dark blue bubbles in the bottom of the image) as a boundary to seperate devices into groups based on their security requirements. If this is the first time you are seeing ACI, you may think of EGP as a VLAN. There is no communication allowed between EPGs by default. To facilitate communication between EPGs, you must configure contracts (light blue bubbles in the image). CCP has automatically built both the EPGs and the relevant contracts for Kubernetes within ACI using API calls. CCP also configures vCenter with the corresponding port groups via ACI and puts the kubernetes nodes in them.

Step {{ step }} - Check to see if ACI sees the Nodes

Click on kube-nodes, and Operational. The operational tab under an EPG in ACI shows the current active devices that ACI sees from the leaf switch ports. ACI keeps track of both the mac-addresses and IP addresses within the fabric, thus eliminating the need for arp and reducing broadcasts and removing 'flood-and-learn' from the fabric; one of the many advantages of ACI.

Notice that ACI has learned the IP addresses of the nodes from the data plane
